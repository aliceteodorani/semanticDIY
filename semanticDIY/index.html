about

Welcome to Semantic DIY, a website where you can create your own recipes for a range of everyday products, including soaps, detergents, shampoos, and even food. Our platform is called "semantic" because it allows you to modify the recipes based on ingredients you already have at home, finding substitutes for those you don't, and customizing your products to suit your preferences. By using Semantic DIY, you have the power to take control over the products you use every day. You can avoid buying from big companies and contributing to their profits, while also reducing waste and pollution associated with packaging. Our platform empowers you to create sustainable, eco-friendly products that are tailored to your needs. We believe that everyone deserves the opportunity to make a positive impact on the environment and take charge of their own health and well-being. Our mission is to make sustainable living accessible and convenient for everyone, by providing a platform that is easy to use and customize. Join the Semantic DIY community today and start creating your own recipes for a better, more sustainable future.

Fats provide better slicing, it tenderizes baked items and helps in prolonging shelf life. A tablespoon or two of sugar can be a wonderful addition to your bread, it will create a beautiful and delicious caramelization of the crust. YOu will also get a faster rise time, longer shelf life, and an overall moister and lighter loaf. Bread can still be made without adding sugar. However, removing sugar from the recipe will alter its texture, taste, freshness, and speed of the rise. Without sugar, yeast can still multiply by feeding on the naturally occurring sugar and starches in flour.

toothpaste

The basic ingredients for homemade toothpaste are:

baking soda

//

salt

//

water

or

coconut oil

Homemade toothpaste can be just as effective at plaque removal as store-bought toothpaste. After all, any toothpaste serves as an abrasive to help remove plaque and food from tooth surfaces.A key ingredient in toothpaste is sodium chloride, aka table salt. The salt works as a gentle abrasive, helping to remove stains from the teeth, disinfect the mouth and reduce inflammation in the gums. You can add optional thickeners like organic arrowroot powder, flour or cornstach. You can even add powdered spices or other ingredients to give a specific flavour to your product.
